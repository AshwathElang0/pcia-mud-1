# Diagnostic Checklist for Weak Colorimetric Trends

## 1. Biological Factors
- [ ] Is the bacterial culture fresh and in exponential growth phase?
- [ ] Was the inoculum density correct (typically 10^5-10^6 CFU/mL)?
- [ ] Is the incubation temperature optimal (usually 35-37°C)?
- [ ] Are you using a pH indicator dye (resazurin, MTT, etc.)?
- [ ] Check positive control: does growth occur without antibiotic?
- [ ] Check negative control: is medium sterile?

## 2. Imaging Conditions
- [ ] Is lighting consistent across all timepoints?
- [ ] Are you using the same camera settings (exposure, white balance)?
- [ ] Is the camera angle/distance fixed?
- [ ] Any shadows or reflections on the plate?
- [ ] Consider using a lightbox or imaging enclosure

## 3. Timing Issues
- [ ] 25 minutes may be too short for detectable growth
- [ ] Try extending to 45-60 minutes or even 2-4 hours
- [ ] Bacterial doubling time is typically 20-30 minutes

## 4. Concentration Range
- [ ] Is the antibiotic concentration range appropriate?
- [ ] You might be testing concentrations all above or all below the MIC
- [ ] Consider a wider dilution series

## 5. Technical
- [ ] Verify grid detection is consistent across timepoints
- [ ] Check for edge effects or evaporation
- [ ] Ensure mixing was adequate before aliquoting
